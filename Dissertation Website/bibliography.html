Bibliography

All sources used in this dissertation have been listed below. Primary sources were supplied by ScotsCorpus.
The journals were found on the University of Glasgow Library's website. The books by Liddicoat and Sidnell were purchased by myself.


Primary Sources

  • Scottish Corpus of Texts & Speech (2007). Department of English Language, University of Glasgow, Scotland, UK. Copyright for texts remains with individual authors: please see each text for details
  • Corbett, J.B. (1998) Conversation 06: Three Ayrshire sisters reminiscing in ScotsCorpus Glasgow: University of Glasgow, Downloaded 18/11/2014
  • SCOTS Project.(2005) Conversation 16: Four students on memories and music festivals in ScotsCorpus Glasgow: University of Glasgow, Downloaded 18/11/2014
  • Anthony, L. (2014- ). AntConc (Version 3.4.3) [Computer Software]. Tokyo, Japan: Waseda University. Available here

  • Drew, P. (1997) ‘Open-class Repair Initiators in Response to Sequential Sources of Troubles in Conversation in Journal of Pragmatics 28, 69-101

  • Egbert, M.M. (1997) ‘Some Interactional Achievements of Other-Initiated Repair in Multiperson Conversation’ in Journal of Pragmatics, 27:5, 611-634

  • Leech, G. (2014) ‘Politeness and Its ‘Opposites’ in The Pragmatics of Politeness New York: Oxford University Press, 271-274
  • Schegloff, E. Jefferson, G. Sacks, H. (1977) ‘The Preference for Self-Correction in the Organisation of Repair in Conversation’ in Language 53:2, 361-382
  • Svennevig, J. (2008) ‘Trying the Easiest Solution First in Other-Initiation of Repair’ in Journal of Pragmatics 40, 333-348
