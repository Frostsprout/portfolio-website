Introduction

The repair device is a system which is frequently used within a conversation to supply the participants with a set of organised practices (Sidwell 2009:110) to resolve problems that can arise from speaking, hearing or understanding (Sidwell 2009:110).

Liddiecoat (2007) stated that ‘while designed to deal with multiple types of trouble and allowing any participant to initiate repair once s/he has identified a trouble, (repair) is constrained by social as well as linguistic considerations' (Liddiecoat 2007:212). Thus, the aim of this dissertation is to confirm whether or not repairs are affected by the linguistic variables (or considerations) of age and relationship.

This dissertation while analyse the repairs found in a conversation between four young students and a conversation between three mature sisters, in accordance with the preference system, ‘other’-selection of other-initiated repair, and the interpersonal banter principle, to confirm if age and relationship do affect the repairs found.


Research Questions

How do the linguistic variables of age and relationship affect repairs in the group conversations between four young Scottish students compared to three mature Scottish sisters through an analysis of:

1. The preference system of self-repair and other-repair.

2. The unique ‘other’-selection of other-initiated repair.

3. The interpersonal banter principle
